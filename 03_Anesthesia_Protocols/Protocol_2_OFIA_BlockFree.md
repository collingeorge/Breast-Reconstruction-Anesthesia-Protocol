# Protocol 2 — Opioid-Free GA (No Regional Blocks)  
### Post-Mastectomy Breast Implant Revision  
**Needle-Minimizing Pathway** • Version 1.0 — December 2025  

[![License: CC BY 4.0](https://img.shields.io/badge/License-CC%20BY%204.0-lightgrey.svg)](https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by/4.0/)

---

## Why Choose This Pathway?
- **Zero extra needle sticks** (only one IV)  
- True **opioid-free anesthesia**  
- Still dramatically lowers PONV vs conventional GA  
- Ideal for needle-phobic patients or when blocks are contraindicated  
- Relies on enhanced IV multimodal analgesia + robust **surgeon local infiltration**

---

## Indications
- Patient refuses PECS/Serratus blocks  
- Needle phobia / extreme anxiety about injections  
- Coagulopathy or therapeutic anticoagulation  
- Local infection at planned block site  
- High PONV risk but unwilling/unable to receive blocks  
- Surgeon willing to perform generous local infiltration  

---

## Contraindications

| Absolute                              | Relative                                              |
|---------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------|
| Patient refusal of OFA                | Severe COPD / CO₂ retention                           |
| True allergy to all usable agents     | Uncontrolled tachyarrhythmia (ketamine concern)       |
|                                       | Anticipated extremely painful case (e.g., total capsulectomy + ADM reconstruction) |

---

## Preoperative Preparation

| Medication              | Dose                        | Notes                                          |
|-------------------------|-----------------------------|------------------------------------------------|
| Acetaminophen           | 1000 mg PO                  |                                                |
| Celecoxib               | 200–400 mg PO               | If no renal/GI contraindications               |
| Gabapentin (optional)   | 300–600 mg PO               | Especially helpful in anxious patients         |
| Midazolam (optional)    | 1–2 mg IV                   | Use liberally in needle-phobic patients        |
| Antibiotic prophylaxis  | Cefazolin 2–3 g IV          | Or vancomycin/clindamycin if allergic         |

---

## Induction (Opioid-Free, No Block)

| Step | Agent          | Dose                     | Purpose                              |
|------|----------------|--------------------------|--------------------------------------|
| 1    | Lidocaine      | 1–1.5 mg/kg IV           | Blunts sympathetic response          |
| 2    | Propofol       | 1.5–2 mg/kg IV           | Induction                            |
| 3    | Ketamine       | 0.3–0.5 mg/kg IV         | Primary intraoperative analgesic     |
| 4    | Rocuronium     | 0.6–1.0 mg/kg (if ETT)   | Only if ETT planned                  |
| 5    | Airway         | **LMA preferred**        | Smoother emergence                   |

---

## Maintenance

| Component                | Regimen                                           |
|--------------------------|---------------------------------------------------|
| Volatile / TIVA          | Sevoflurane 0.8–1.2 MAC **OR** Propofol 75–125 mcg/kg/min |
| Dexmedetomidine          | 0.2–0.6 mcg/kg/hr (**no bolus**)                  |
| Lidocaine infusion       | 1–1.5 mg/kg/hr (max 2 mg/kg/hr)                   |
| Ventilation              | TV 6–8 mL/kg IBW, ETCO₂ 32–38 mmHg                |
| Hemodynamics             | SBP >90 mmHg → phenylephrine/ephedrine preferred  |

**Critical:** Surgeon must infiltrate generously with 0.25–0.5% bupivacaine (± epinephrine) — this is the cornerstone of analgesia in Protocol 2.

---

## Intraoperative Analgesia Boosters (No Block)

| Situation                         | Intervention                              |
|-----------------------------------|-------------------------------------------|
| Pocket entry / capsulectomy       | Ketamine 10–20 mg IV bolus                |
| Implant manipulation              | Ketamine 5–10 mg IV                       |
| Persistent tachycardia/hypertension| Increase dexmedetomidine rate slightly   |
| End of case                       | Ketorolac 15–30 mg IV (if no contraindication) |

---

## PONV Prophylaxis (Give to Everyone)
- Ondansetron 4 mg IV  
- Dexamethasone 8 mg IV  
- Scopolamine patch (highly effective in needle-phobic patients with PONV history)  
- **No nitrous oxide**  

---

## Emergence
1. Stop dexmedetomidine → 10–15 min before end  
2. Stop lidocaine infusion  
3. Reverse NMB (sugammadex preferred)  
4. Remove LMA/ETT when following commands  
→ Expected pain **3–6/10** on arrival (still very manageable)

---

## PACU Orders (Non-Opioid First)
- Acetaminophen 1000 mg IV/PO q6h  
- Ketorolac 15–30 mg IV q6h × 48h (or ibuprofen 400–600 mg PO)  
- Ice packs to chest  
- Ondansetron 4 mg IV PRN  
- **Opioids only if pain >6/10 and refractory to above**
