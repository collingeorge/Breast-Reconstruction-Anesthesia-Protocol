# Protocol 3 — Conventional Opioid-Based General Anesthesia (No Blocks)  
### Post-Mastectomy Breast Implant Revision  
**Fallback / Traditional Pathway** • Version 1.0 — December 2025  

[![License: CC BY 4.0](https://img.shields.io/badge/License-CC%20BY%204.0-lightgrey.svg)](https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by/4.0/)

---

## When to Use This Pathway
This is the **traditional** general anesthesia approach and is appropriate when:

- Patient declines both OFA and regional blocks  
- Chronic opioid use or high opioid tolerance  
- Regional blocks contraindicated AND OFA not preferred  
- Patient preference for "standard anesthesia"  
- Severe anxiety requiring deeper sedation  
- Complex or salvage revision expected  

---

## Indications
- Opioid tolerance or chronic opioid therapy  
- OFA contraindicated (severe psychiatric illness, complex pain syndrome)  
- Patient refuses opioid-free care  
- Anticipated severe surgical pain  
- Salvage cases with significant scarring or tissue loss  

---

## Contraindications

| Absolute                    | Relative                                           |
|-----------------------------|----------------------------------------------------|
| None (universally usable)   | Very high PONV risk (consider multimodal rescue)   |
|                             | Severe OSA (minimize opioids / consider LMA)       |
|                             | Poorly controlled GERD (strong ETT preference)     |

---

## Required Equipment
- Standard ASA monitors  
- Airway: **ETT preferred**, though LMA acceptable for low-risk cases  
- IV opioid availability  
- Antiemetics (minimum two classes required)  
- Infusion pumps for propofol/sevoflurane maintenance  

---

# **Preoperative Preparation**

| Medication       | Dose & Notes                                   |
|------------------|-------------------------------------------------|
| Acetaminophen    | 1000 mg PO                                      |
| NSAID            | Celecoxib 200 mg PO (if no contraindication)    |
| Gabapentin       | 300 mg PO (optional for anxiety/pain)           |
| Antibiotics      | Cefazolin 2–3 g IV (<60 min pre-incision)       |

**Note:** Pre-op multimodal analgesia still improves outcomes even in opioid-based GA.

---

# **Induction (Opioid-Based)**

| Step | Agent          | Dose / Range                   | Purpose                                      |
|------|----------------|--------------------------------|----------------------------------------------|
| 1    | Fentanyl       | 1–2 mcg/kg IV                  | Analgesia for induction                      |
| 2    | Lidocaine      | 1 mg/kg IV                     | Blunts sympathetic response                  |
| 3    | Propofol       | 1.5–2 mg/kg IV                 | Induction                                    |
| 4    | Rocuronium     | 0.6–1.0 mg/kg IV               | Muscle relaxation                            |
| 5    | Airway         | ETT (preferred) or LMA         | Secure airway                                |

**Note:** Hydromorphone 0.2–0.4 mg IV may be given for longer cases pre-incision.

---

# **Maintenance**

| Component            | Regimen                                       |
|----------------------|-----------------------------------------------|
| Volatile agent       | Sevoflurane 1–2% (typical)                    |
| Opioids              | Fentanyl 25–50 mcg IV PRN Q10–20 min          |
| Hydromorphone        | 0.2–0.4 mg IV PRN (sparingly)                 |
| Ventilation          | ETCO₂ 32–38 mmHg, TV 6–8 mL/kg IBW            |
| Hemodynamics         | Treat hypotension with phenylephrine/ephedrine|
| Local infiltration   | Strongly encouraged (bupivacaine ± epi)       |

### Notes:
- Target lowest effective opioid dose to avoid oversedation.  
- Limit total fentanyl > 250–300 mcg to avoid prolonged emergence.  
- Consider dexmedetomidine *low dose only* if patient becoming tachycardic or anxious (optional).

---

# **PONV Prophylaxis (Mandatory for Opioid-Based GA)**

Patients receiving opioids must receive **at least 2 antiemetic classes**:

- **Ondansetron** 4 mg IV  
- **Dexamethasone** 4–8 mg IV  
- ± Scopolamine patch (high-risk)  
- ± Droperidol 0.625 mg IV  
- Avoid nitrous oxide if possible  

PONV rate typically **20–40%** if not aggressively treated.

---

# **Emergence**

1. Stop volatile anesthetic  
2. Reverse NMB  
3. Suction oropharynx  
4. Extubate when awake, following commands  
5. Prepare for moderate discomfort (pain score 5–7/10 common)

**Post-op opioid rescue acceptable** but titrate cautiously.

---

# **PACU Orders**

### Analgesia:
- Acetaminophen q6h  
- NSAID (ibuprofen/ketorolac if appropriate)  
- Hydromorphone 0.2–0.4 mg IV PRN  
- Oxycodone 5–10 mg PO PRN  
- Ice to chest  
- Gabapentin 100–300 mg qHS (optional)

### Nausea:
- Ondansetron 4 mg IV PRN  
- Promethazine 12.5 mg IV PRN  
- Or Scopolamine if not already placed
