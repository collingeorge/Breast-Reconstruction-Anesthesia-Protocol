# Protocol 3 — Conventional Opioid-Based General Anesthesia (No Blocks)  
### Post-Mastectomy Breast Implant Revision  
**Fallback / Traditional Pathway** • Version 1.0 — December 2025

---

## When to Use This Pathway
The reliable, universally accepted “classic” GA technique. Select when:
- Patient **declines** both OFA and regional blocks  
- Chronic opioid use / high opioid tolerance  
- Regional blocks contraindicated **and** OFA declined  
- Patient explicitly wants “standard anesthesia”  
- Salvage or highly complex revision anticipated  
- Severe anxiety requiring deeper, opioid-supported sedation  

---

## Indications & Contraindications

| Indications                              | Contraindications (Relative)                     |
|------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------|
| Chronic opioid therapy                   | Very high PONV risk (Apfel = 4) → reconsider OFA |
| Patient refusal of opioid-free care      | Severe untreated OSA → minimize opioids          |
| OFA contraindicated (psychiatric, etc.) | Poorly controlled GERD → prefer ETT + rapid sequence |
| Anticipated severe surgical stimulation  |                                                  |

## Preoperative Preparation

| Medication             | Dose                          | Notes                                      |
|------------------------|-------------------------------|--------------------------------------------|
| Acetaminophen          | 1000 mg PO                    | Always give                                |
| Midazolam (optional)   | 1–2 mg IV                     | Anxiety, memory inhibitor. Use liberally in needle-phobic patients    |
| Celecoxib              | 200–400 mg PO                 | If no renal/GI contraindications           |
| Gabapentin (optional)  | 300–600 mg PO                 | Helpful for anxiety & neuropathic pain     |
| Antibiotic prophylaxis | Cefazolin 2–3 g IV            | Or vancomycin/clindamycin if allergic      |

---

## Induction (Opioid-Based)

| Step | Agent          | Dose                        | Purpose                              |
|------|----------------|-----------------------------|--------------------------------------|
| 1    | Fentanyl       | 1–2 mcg/kg IV               | Analgesia & hemodynamic smoothing    |
| 2    | Lidocaine      | 1 mg/kg IV                  | Attenuates pressor response          |
| 3    | Propofol       | 1.5–2 mg/kg IV              | Induction                            |
| 4    | Rocuronium     | 0.6–1.0 mg/kg IV            | Muscle relaxation                    |
| 5    | Airway         | **ETT preferred**, LMA acceptable |                                      |

**Optional:** Hydromorphone 0.2–0.4 mg IV just before incision for longer cases.

---

## Maintenance

| Component               | Regimen                                              |
|-------------------------|------------------------------------------------------|
| Volatile agent          | Sevoflurane 0.8–2% (titrate to BIS 40–60 if used)    |
| Opioid supplementation  | Fentanyl 25–50 mcg IV q10–20 min PRN<br>Hydromorphone 0.2–0.4 mg IV PRN |
| Local infiltration      | **Strongly encouraged** by surgeon (0.25–0.5% bupivacaine ± epinephrine) |
| Ventilation             | TV 6–8 mL/kg IBW, ETCO₂ 32–38 mmHg                   |
| Hemodynamics            | Treat hypotension with phenylephrine/ephedrine       |

**Goal:** Use the **lowest effective opioid dose** to avoid delayed emergence and excessive PONV.

---

## PONV Prophylaxis (Mandatory & Aggressive)

**Minimum two agents** (three if Apfel ≥3):

- Ondansetron 4 mg IV  
- Dexamethasone 8 mg IV  
- ± Scopolamine patch (place pre-op if high risk)  
- ± Droperidol 0.625 mg IV (excellent rescue)  
- **Avoid nitrous oxide**

Expected PONV rate with opioids: **20–40%** if not aggressively prophylaxed.

---

## Emergence
1. Discontinue volatile agent  
2. Reverse neuromuscular blockade (sugammadex preferred)  
3. Suction oropharynx  
4. Extubate awake and following commands  
→ Anticipate **moderate discomfort** on arrival (pain 5–7/10 common)

---

## PACU Orders

### Analgesia (non-opioid first)
- Acetaminophen 1000 mg IV/PO q6h  
- Ketorolac 15–30 mg IV q6h × 48h **or** ibuprofen 400–600 mg PO  
- Ice packs to chest  

### Opioid rescue (titrate carefully)
- Hydromorphone 0.2–0.4 mg IV q10–15 min PRN  
- Oxycodone 5–10 mg PO PRN once tolerating liquids  

### Nausea rescue
- Ondansetron 4 mg IV q6h PRN  
- Promethazine 6.25–12.5 mg IV q6h PRN  
- Scopolamine patch if not already placed
